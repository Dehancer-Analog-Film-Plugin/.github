## 🚀 What It Is
Dehancer for Windows is a film emulation plugin built around the way analog film really looks and behaves. Rather than applying a flat preset, it reproduces the color response of specific film stocks, layers on organic grain, and adds the halation and bloom that make a film image feel alive. It plugs straight into supported video editors and color-grading applications, so you shape a genuine analog film look as a natural part of your grade.

> Dehancer treats film as an optical process, not a filter — you grade through a print-and-negative pipeline for a look that reads as truly photographic.

## ✨ Key Features
- 🎞️ **Real film profiles** — Match the color and contrast of classic analog film stocks sampled from actual material.
- 🌾 **Organic grain** — Layer authentic scanned grain that responds to exposure and image detail.
- 💡 **Halation and bloom** — Add the warm glow and highlight spill that give film its signature softness.
- 🖨️ **Print and negative pipeline** — Work through a negative-to-print flow for an optically accurate film emulation.
- 🔌 **Plugin integration** — Runs inside popular editors and grading apps so nothing leaves your timeline.

## 🎯 First Steps
1. Load a project in your supported host app and drop a clip onto the timeline.
2. Add Dehancer as an effect or node on the clip.
3. Pick a film profile to establish the base analog look.
4. Adjust grain, halation, and bloom, then refine the print and negative settings.
5. Toggle before and after to check the result, then export your graded footage.

## ❓ Questions
**Is Dehancer free?**
Dehancer is a paid plugin with a free trial. The trial gives you full access to the film emulation tools so you can test them in your own projects, and buying a license lifts the trial restrictions. There is no free complete edition, and this project neither supplies nor endorses any means of circumventing licensing.

**What is Dehancer, exactly?**
Dehancer is a film emulation plugin that rebuilds the analog film look from real film stocks, grain, halation, bloom, and a print/negative color workflow. It runs inside your editor or color-grading software so the film look becomes part of your grade instead of a separate export.

**Is Dehancer worth it?**
That depends on how much you value an authentic analog film look. If you regularly grade for a cinematic, photographic result, many users find the accuracy of Dehancer's film emulation and its print workflow worth the cost. For occasional or purely stylized work, a lighter tool may be enough — the free trial is the honest way to judge fit before buying, and this README makes no fabricated claims about ratings.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
Dehancer for Windows works on current 64-bit Windows releases, generally Windows 10 and Windows 11, alongside a supported host editing or grading application.
